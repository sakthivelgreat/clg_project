Chi-Square Test of Independence

The Chi-Square test of independence tests if there is a significant association between two categorical variables.

\[ \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O_i - E_i)^2}{E_i} \]

where:

  • - \( O_i \) is the observed frequency.
  • - \( E_i \) is the expected frequency.









Chi-Square Test of Independence

The Chi-Square test of independence tests if there is a significant association between two categorical variables.

The formula for the Chi-Square test statistic is:

\[ \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O_i - E_i)^2}{E_i} \]

where: - \( O_i \) is the observed frequency. - \( E_i \) is the expected frequency.

The degrees of freedom for the Chi-Square test of independence is:

\[ df = (r - 1)(c - 1) \]
