One-Way ANOVA

One-Way ANOVA tests if there are any statistically significant differences between the means of three or more independent groups.

**Null Hypothesis (H0)**: All group means are equal (\(\mu_1 = \mu_2 = \mu_3 = \cdots = \mu_k\)).

**Alternative Hypothesis (H1)**: At least one group mean is different from the others.

The formula for the F-statistic in ANOVA is:

\[ F = \frac{\text{MSB}}{\text{MSW}} \]

where:

\[ \text{MSB} = \frac{\text{SSB}}{\text{df}_B} \]

\[ \text{MSW} = \frac{\text{SSW}}{\text{df}_W} \]

\[ \text{SSB} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} n_i (\bar{X}_i - \bar{X})^2 \]

\[ \text{SSW} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} \sum_{j=1}^{n_i} (X_{ij} - \bar{X}_i)^2 \]

\[ \text{df}_B = k - 1 \]

\[ \text{df}_W = N - k \]
