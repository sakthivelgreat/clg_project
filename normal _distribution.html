FITTING A NORMAL DISTRIBUTION:-

Normal Distribution

The normal distribution, also known as the bell curve, describes how data points are distributed around a mean (average) value. It is symmetric, meaning it looks the same on both sides of the mean. Most values cluster around the mean, with fewer values farther away, creating a bell shape.

f(X=x)= 1/ [σ √2π ] e - [(x-μ) 2/ 2σ 2]

where,


  • μ: mean
  • σ: standard deviation
