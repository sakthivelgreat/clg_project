CORRELATION :-

Pearson correlation

The Pearson correlation coefficient measures how closely two sets of data points cluster around a straight line: +1 indicates a perfect positive linear relationship, -1 a perfect negative one, and 0 no linear relationship. As denoted by

\[ r = \frac{n \sum xy - (\sum x)(\sum y)}{\sqrt{[n \sum x^2 - (\sum x)^2][n \sum y^2 - (\sum y)^2]}} \]

spearman rank correlation

The Spearman Rank Correlation Coefficient, often denoted as ρ or r s is a non-parametric measure of the strength and direction of the association between two ranked variables.

The formula for the Spearman Rank Correlation Coefficient is:

\[ \text{r}_s = 1- \frac{6 \sum d_i^2 }{n(n^2 -1)} \]
